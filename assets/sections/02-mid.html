Exhibit 04 What aiCRO does

The deliverable a firm hands over — at a prompt's speed, with every number source-tagged and a standing auditor re-multiplying the deal math.

Each benefit below is a before → after, not a feature. The proof sits in the same card as the claim — and where the claim is evidence-grading, the card shows a real tag.

Consulting-grade, not slop

From a number you quietly discount to one a client can trust.

A raw prompt invents numbers and hallucinates competitors with no citable URL. aiCRO produces a positioning map, deal math, a phased roadmap, and a rebuilt site — structured, cited, and finished, not prose that embarrasses you in front of a client.

Ends in a finished site

From a deck that describes what to do to a site you publish.

A firm — and a prompt — stops at advice: a to-do list the owner ignores. aiCRO ends at a rebuilt site that ships ready to publish, with the reasoning beside every section. Not "rewrite your hero," but here is your new hero.

Evidence-graded

From an AI number you discount to a number that shows its work.

Every figure ships tagged or it gets cut, and a standing arithmetic auditor re-multiplies every deal-math strip. A deal-math number ships as $8,400 [measured], never a bare figure; every named company carries a citable URL.

Self-contained

From "read the README on the server" to a folder you email.

Zero server, zero CDN, zero webfonts, zero frameworks; system fonts only. The whole engagement opens from file://, makes zero network requests, survives zip → email → unzip, and nothing phones home.

Done-with-you

From 40–80 hours of analysis to paste-ready copy — with your hand on the publish button.

aiCRO is not an agency and refuses to become one. It removes the analysis, framework-sequencing, and assembly. What it deliberately leaves you is the publish button — because no operator wants an AI pushing live changes to a client's site unsupervised. The last mile isn't leftover work; it's the control you'd never give up.

Exhibit 05 The pipeline

Four phases, one human gate. Starting takes a URL.

aiCRO is a four-phase pipeline, not a prompt you re-run hopefully. Agents read from disk, write to disk, and pass manifests — so the engagement is reproducible, and nothing inside a phase needs babysitting.

Hand a client a rebuilt website with the reasoning beside every section — not an audit they'll ignore.

Point aiCRO at a URL and it produces five self-contained artifacts per engagement, under one folder. The flagship is the website: the rebuilt site rendered inside a simulated browser, with a scroll-synced guidance sidebar that argues every decision and cites the analysis behind it.

One page: the one-number diagnosis, a 20-minute action path, and the deliverable map. The only file you have to tell the client about.

 The book

Cover, numbered chapters, captioned exhibits, appendix, and glossary — print-quality, assembled into one file.

 The plan

Phase −1 (access) through Phase 4 (demand gen). Every task has an owner, a minute count, a day, and a click-path. Persistent checkboxes.

URL bar, tabs, device toggle — with a scroll-synced guidance sidebar explaining what each section does and why, citing the book. The raw site also ships standalone, ready to publish.

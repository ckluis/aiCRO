What moves them, what holds them back03 / 12

The four forces

Every switch is a tug-of-war: the pain of today and the pull of the new pushing toward Marigold, the comfort of the current habit and the fear of a bad outcome pulling back. The job of the page and the campaign is to grow the first two and shrink the last two.

Top three objections, answered

"It's more than the app I've been using."

True — and on purpose. The app sends a different 1099 contractor each visit; Marigold sends the same W-2 team that learns your home and stands behind the work with a 24-hour re-clean. You're paying for consistency and accountability, not a one-time scrub.

"I don't want to be locked into a contract."

There isn't one. Skip or reschedule any visit free, and stop whenever you like. The plan is a rhythm, not a commitment — most households stay because it works, not because they're trapped (94% are still with us past six months).

"How do I know who's coming into my home?"

Every cleaner is a background-checked W-2 employee — trained, equipped, and insured by Marigold — not a gig-app stranger. You're matched with one small, named team that returns each visit, so over time you actually know the people you trust with a key.

Where the effort goes10 / 12

Channels — bullseye

Concentrate on the few channels where high-intent households already are, and be honest about what to skip. The booking page is the landing page for all of it.

Skip, on purpose

Skip: broad social/display brand ads — this group decides on intent, not impressions; the spend converts far better against people already searching.
Skip: daily-deal / coupon sites — they pre-select for one-time bargain hunters, the opposite of a household that stays for years, and they undercut the premium trust position.
Skip: a relaunched discount popup — the old "$25 off" modal with a dead code did real damage; if a first-clean incentive runs at all, it's one honest live line near the CTA, never an on-load interrupt.

Metrics & the 30-day check

What this deck commits to

The north-star is net new recurring households per month — not total bookings — because one steady household is worth roughly twelve one-time cleans a year, and only recurring compounds the business. Everything in this deck is aimed at that one number and the retention behind it.

The 30-day binary check: by day 30 after the booking flow is live, has Marigold confirmed its first self-serve recurring booking with no callback in the path? Yes → the mechanics work; scale the channels. No → fix the flow before spending on traffic.

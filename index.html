Exhibit 03 The problem

Five sub-30-minute fixes sat undone for 14 months. The governing fact behind aiCRO — the constraint was never insight. It was owner time and activation energy.

The audit isn’t the hard part. Getting it acted on is.

A real growth engagement — positioning, ICPs, a sales motion, a rebuilt site, per-segment GTM — takes a skilled human 40–80 hours and a deep bench of frameworks. The people who need it most can’t afford that. The people who could produce it can’t produce it fast enough to be worth their time.

So the real problem is two-sided: producing the engagement is too slow and expensive, and the engagement that gets produced doesn’t get acted on. The audits that do get made overwhelmingly get ignored.

Each escape hatch fails its own way. Doing it yourself assumes 80 hours and framework fluency you don’t have. A raw AI prompt is fast, but it invents numbers, hallucinates competitors with no citable URL, and reads like slop that embarrasses you in front of a client. And a firm is $10K–$50K and weeks of calendar time — and the deliverable is usually a deck that describes what to do, not a finished site you publish. It still ends in a to-do list the owner ignores.

Exhibit 07 Straight answers

The three things you’re right to ask first.

No hedging, no “we recommend.” The straight answer to each, and the discipline shown rather than claimed — because the page should grade its own claims the way the engine grades a deliverable.

“This is just AI slop with a nice template.”

Open a real engagement and check the arithmetic yourself. Every number is source-tagged; every named company has a citable URL; a standing arithmetic auditor re-multiplies the deal math. And read the refusal list: no SWOT or PESTLE theater, no “this program is worth $200K” upside multiples, no unverifiable attributions. A template can’t refuse those.

[measured] [benchmark] [estimate] [verify before publishing]

Every figure ships carrying one of these, or it gets cut. The fourth gates any flattering claim about the client until it’s confirmed.

“Will it actually work for my client / industry?”

The output reads your site, not a template — it’s per-site and per-segment. The demo engagement is a premium home-cleaning company: a rebuilt site shown inside a guided preview, five customer groups with deal math, with exactly one funded as Primary — and a paste-ready go-to-market kit for that Primary group, a consulting book, and a 90-day plan. It works because it analyzes the actual business, not a category average. Open it — the specificity is the proof.

“How much effort is this — will the client even act on it?”

Starting takes a URL. The output takes zero setup to consume — it opens from file://, no install, no server, no account. And the whole thing is built backwards from will a busy owner act on this in 20 minutes? — that’s the cover’s 20-minute path and the plan’s per-task minute counts and click-paths. aiCRO’s founding fact is that adoption, not analysis, is the constraint; the deliverables are shaped around it.

Not for someone who wants a one-click report they’ll never open. Not done-for-you — aiCRO ends at paste-ready, you ship. And not for the hands-off skeptic who won’t open the sample: the strongest proof is a real engagement you can inspect, and if you won’t look, no testimonial will move you.

Exhibit 08 Versus the alternatives

DIY can’t. A firm is slow and stops at a deck. A raw prompt ships slop. Skill packs are parts.

Readers compare, and one-sided claims lose — so this says what each alternative is genuinely better at. aiCRO’s edge is narrow and specific: grade-able, shippable, done-with-you output in a day, not the month a bespoke engagement costs.

Who each is best for

DIY is best for the operator who has 80 spare hours and real framework fluency, and wants total control of every call. A firm is best when you need a named human’s judgment and accountability on the hook — that’s worth $10K–$50K and weeks, and it’s a thing aiCRO genuinely doesn’t give you. A generic prompt is best when you want a free, instant first draft and the stakes are low enough that invented numbers and a missing structure don’t matter. Skill packs are best when you’re already the consultant and just want sharper individual tools — a copywriting skill, a CRO skill — to sequence yourself.

aiCRO wins for one specific profile: the operator who needs to produce consulting-grade engagements repeatably without burning a month each — and who will act on the output rather than file it.
